# Reserve AI — Design Context

## Users

**Primary audience**: global B2B companies looking to integrate AI into their products and operations — invited to the site as a first impression.

**Secondary audience**: B2B2C / B2C consumers of Reserve's own hardware products that bridge AI and the physical world (smart rings, social rings, and future ambient devices).

**Context of use**: visitors arrive via a direct invitation — this is not a discovery surface, it is an *arrival*. They are decision-makers (founders, heads of product, CTOs, investors) evaluating whether Reserve is the kind of studio they want to work with. The site itself is the first demo — if it feels warm, precise, and quietly confident, that is the pitch.

**Job to be done**: understand, within 30 seconds, that Reserve is a rare kind of studio — one that treats technology as a means, not a performance. Leave wanting to reply to the invitation.

## Brand Personality

**Three words**: *warm · composed · human*.

**Voice**: sparse, English, each sentence short and weighted — written in the register of an Aman brochure or a Swedish industrial product page. Nothing explained twice. Whitespace does the heavy lifting.

**Emotional goals**: the visitor should feel they have stepped out of the noisy internet into a quieter room. Relief, curiosity, trust. Never dazzled. Never sold to.

**Craft attitude**: Ghibli-like in its warmth and hand-made texture, Azuki-like in aesthetic rigor, Aman-like in restraint. Technology is present but never worn on the sleeve — the site itself is the proof.

## Aesthetic Direction

**Visual tone**: warm, clean, lo-fi. Cream paper, soft daylight, organic textures (paper grain, gentle gradients, hand-shaped clouds, ink). No chrome, no glass, no neon. If the visitor notices the technology, we failed — they should only notice the mood.

**Theme**: light. Warm daylight (cream / bone / moss / sun-gold), with moments of deeper warm shadow (ink brown, muted indigo) for contrast sections. No true dark mode. No pure black, no pure white.

**References (directional)**:
- Aman Resorts — restraint, poetic captions, generous whitespace
- Azuki — extreme aesthetic commitment, anime-as-craft
- Swedish industrial product pages (Teenage Engineering, Nothing-adjacent) — quiet technical confidence
- Studio Ghibli — warmth, hand, weather, hills

**Anti-references**: no dashboard/SaaS vibes, no admin UI tropes, no cyber/web3 aesthetics, no crypto neon, no sparklines, no glassmorphism, no gradient text, no stiff grid of identical feature cards, no "AI slop" (purple-blue gradients, glowing accents on dark). Nothing that looks transactional.

**Locations**: London · New York · Bangkok · Taipei · Tokyo · Singapore · Hong Kong · UAE. Treat these as a constellation, not a footer afterthought — they are part of the mood.

## Design Principles

1. **Technology is implicit, never performed.** The site is technically ambitious underneath; the surface stays calm. No tech jargon, no stack logos, no product screenshots forced into hero slots.
2. **Every word earns its place.** English only. Short. Poetic by economy, not ornament. If a sentence does not make the visitor pause, cut it.
3. **Weather over layout.** Rhythm comes from light, texture, and air — not from cards and dividers. Break the grid deliberately; let sections breathe asymmetrically.
4. **Warmth is the accent.** 60% cream / paper, 30% ink and moss, 10% sun-gold. Accents are rare and meaningful. No secondary decorative color.
5. **One memorable gesture per section.** Not five micro-animations — one well-made moment. Motion is soft, exponential, never bouncy. Reduced-motion users get the same composition, still.
