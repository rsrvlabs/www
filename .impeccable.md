## Users

**Primary audience**: global B2B companies looking to integrate AI into their products and operations — invited to the site as a first impression.

**Secondary audience**: B2B2C / B2C consumers of Reserve's own hardware products that bridge AI and the physical world (smart rings, social rings, and future ambient devices).

**Context of use**: visitors arrive via a direct invitation — this is not a discovery surface, it is an *arrival*. They are decision-makers (founders, heads of product, CTOs, investors) evaluating whether Reserve is the kind of studio they want to work with. The site itself is the first demo — if it feels warm, precise, and quietly confident, that is the pitch.

**Job to be done**: understand, within 30 seconds, that Reserve is a rare kind of studio — one that treats technology as a means, not a performance. Leave wanting to reply to the invitation.

## Brand Personality

**Three words**: *warm · composed · human*.

**Voice**: sparse, English, each sentence short and weighted. Nothing explained twice. Whitespace does the heavy lifting.

**Emotional goals**: the visitor should feel they have stepped out of the noisy internet into a quieter room. Relief, curiosity, trust. Never dazzled. Never sold to.

**Craft attitude**: restraint over performance. Technology is present but never worn on the sleeve — the site itself is the proof.

## Aesthetic Direction

**Visual tone**: **Apple design language.** Light ground, very large tight-tracked headlines, generous whitespace, one blue accent, surfaces separated by fill and air rather than strokes. Calm and declarative. The page should read like a company reporting a fact, not selling one.

**Theme**: light. Derived from context, not defaulted — decision-makers scan this in daylight on a laptop, and the claim being made is clarity.

**Palette** (tokens in `src/components/apple/apple.module.css`): ink `#1d1d1f` · soft ink `#6e6e73` (captions, meta and footer ONLY, never body copy) · tint `#f5f5f7` · white ground · one blue `#0071e3` used for links and a single pill CTA. Roughly 60% ground / 30% ink / 10% accent by visual weight.

**Imagery leads where imagery exists.** `/lime` carries real device captures and is the strongest page precisely because of them. (This reverses the earlier "no product screenshots in hero slots" rule, which belonged to the cream direction.) Type-and-boxes is the fallback, not the target.

**References (directional)**: apple.com product and marketing pages — the type scale, the section rhythm, the cardless feature rows, the restraint with colour.

**Anti-references**:
- **Material Design.** The first Apple pass copied Apple's *palette* onto a Material *component grammar* — ALL-CAPS wide-tracked overlines, coloured eyebrows, 1px-stroked cards, grey body copy, centred card text, symmetric 3-up card grids — and the founder rejected it on sight: *"很 material design ui...這件事情很不好"*. The grammar rules that came out of that are in `DESIGN.md` and are binding.
- The superseded warm-cream and dark-exhibition directions above.
- No dashboard / SaaS tropes, no admin UI, no cyber or web3 aesthetics, no sparklines, no glassmorphism, no gradient text, no grid of identical feature cards, no AI slop (purple-blue gradients, glowing accents on dark). Nothing that looks transactional.

**Language**: **English only** in the UI — no CJK anywhere on the site (founder: 不要有中文字). Team-facing artefacts (tickets, PRs, commit bodies) stay zh-TW.

**Locations**: London · New York · Bangkok · Taipei · Tokyo · Singapore · Hong Kong · UAE. A constellation, not a footer afterthought.

## Design Principles

1. **Technology is implicit, never performed.** The site is technically ambitious underneath; the surface stays calm. No stack logos, no jargon.
2. **Every word earns its place.** Short. Economical rather than ornamental. If a sentence does not make the visitor pause, cut it.
3. **Evidence over adjectives.** Every section should name something that actually runs. If a claim cannot be pointed at, cut it rather than dress it.
4. **The grammar is the language, not the palette.** Matching a reference's colours while keeping another system's components is the failure mode this project has already hit once.
5. **One job per section**, and one memorable gesture per section — not five micro-animations. Motion is soft and exponential, never bouncy. Reduced-motion users get the same composition, still.

## Known open gap

Company-level pages (`/`, `/labs`, `/frontiers`, `/effects`, `/research`) carry **no imagery at all**. Apple's pages are image-led, so this is the largest remaining distance between the site and its stated target. It needs source material, not CSS. Open founder decision as of 2026-07-23.
